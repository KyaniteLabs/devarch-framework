Market Positioning

Unique Value Proposition

Achiote is not a recipe app. It is a food memory detective that uses forensic culinary analysis to reconstruct half-remembered dishes from fragmented descriptions, sensory memories, and family folklore. Unlike generic AI recipe chatbots, Achiote combines:

Not This

Recipe generator, meal planner, grocery delivery, cooking tutorial platform, generic AI chatbot with food knowledge

But This

Food-memory archaeologist, culinary genealogy service, nostalgia preservation platform, structured family documentation tool

Target Segments

Diaspora Families (Primary)

Second/third-generation immigrants seeking to reconnect with cultural heritage through grandmother's recipes. High emotional motivation, strong word-of-mouth potential within communities.

Culinary Genealogists (Secondary)

Food historians, cookbook authors, and culinary researchers who need systematic documentation of family recipes with provenance tracking and citation support.

Grief & Loss Community (Tertiary)

Bereavement support groups, hospice organizations, and families processing loss through food memory preservation. High willingness to pay, strong emotional resonance.

Cultural Preservation Orgs (Enterprise)

Museums, cultural centers, and ancestry platforms seeking structured food-memory documentation tools for community archival projects.

Growth Engine

The Viral Loop

Built-In Virality: Family Followup Questions

Achiote's "family followup question generator" isn't just a feature -- it's the growth mechanism. After reconstructing a recipe, the AI generates specific questions to ask family members:

"Ask your aunt: Did Grandma use fresh or dried oregano?"
"Ask your cousin: Do you remember if she soaked the beans overnight?"

Result: User shares questions with family → family gets curious about project → user shares Achiote results → family signs up to reconstruct their own memories.

Single Developer Bus Factor

Low
Critical

Mitigation: Comprehensive documentation (priority), agent workflow documentation, backup developer contract in place, knowledge sharing in public repos reduces lock-in.

Low
